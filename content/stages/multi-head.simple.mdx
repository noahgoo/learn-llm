One attention head is one way of looking. Multi-head attention runs many
of those looks in parallel, then combines them into one update for each
token.<Cite id="vaswani2017" />

That matters because a sentence has many relationships at once. One head
might focus on nearby words, another on repeated structure, another on the
token that resolves a reference. The gallery shows those head patterns
side by side for your text.

The important idea is not that every head has a neat English job. It is
that the model gets several chances to ask different questions before it
writes back to the stream.
