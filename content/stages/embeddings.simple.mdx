A token ID is just a dictionary position — the number 3797 says nothing
about what " cat" *means*. The embedding step gives each token meaning:
the model looks up its row in a giant learned table and gets back a long
list of numbers — a point in a high-dimensional space.

Every LLM does this. During training, tokens used in similar ways drift
close together in that space: " cat" ends up near " dog" and " kitten",
" Paris" near " London". Meaning becomes geometry — directions and
distances you can measure.

The cloud around you is the live model's actual embedding space, flattened
to 3-D so we can fly through it. GPT-2 small uses 768 numbers per token,
while frontier models use far wider vectors.<Cite id="radford2019" /> Your
tokens light up at their real positions — see what the model considers
their neighbors.
