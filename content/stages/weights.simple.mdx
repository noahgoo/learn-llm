The architecture is the recipe: layers, attention heads, MLPs, residual
paths, and normalization. The weights are the learned numbers that make
that recipe useful.<Cite id="vaswani2017" />

Two models can have the same architecture but behave completely
differently if their weights differ. Random weights are just machinery
without learned memory. Trained weights contain the statistical patterns
learned from text.<Cite id="radford2019" />

That is the final split: an LLM is not only code, and not only data. It is
a fixed computation filled with learned parameters.
