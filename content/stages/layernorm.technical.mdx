LayerNorm normalizes across the feature dimension of one token: subtract
the token's mean activation, divide by its standard deviation, then apply
learned scale and bias parameters.<Cite id="ba2016" />

GPT-2 uses a pre-LN block layout: normalization happens before attention
and before the MLP sublayer reads the residual stream.<Cite id="radford2019" />
Pre-LN variants are known to improve optimization behavior in deeper
Transformers compared with the original post-LN placement.<Cite id="xiong2020" />

Many modern open-weight LLMs replace LayerNorm with RMSNorm, which drops
mean subtraction and keeps only root-mean-square rescaling plus learned
scale parameters. The goal is the same stable residual stream with less
normalization work per token.<Cite id="zhang2019" />

The visual keeps the operation local to one token vector, because LayerNorm
does not mix information across sequence positions.
