Each head projects the (normalized) residual stream with learned
matrices: **Q = XW<sub>Q</sub>**, **K = XW<sub>K</sub>**,
**V = XW<sub>V</sub>**. The output is

**Attention(Q, K, V) = softmax(QKᵀ / √d<sub>k</sub> + M) V**

— raw affinities QKᵀ are scaled by √d<sub>k</sub> (without it, dot
products grow with head dimension and push softmax into
vanishing-gradient saturation<Cite id="vaswani2017" />). In decoder-only
LLMs — GPT, Claude, Llama alike — the causal mask **M** sets every
position j &gt; i to −∞, so softmax assigns the future exactly zero
weight.<Cite id="radford2019" />

Each softmax row is a probability distribution — the live matrix you're
viewing, from the model in your browser (d<sub>k</sub> = 64 here); rows
sum to 1. It multiplies V to give each position a convex combination of
value vectors, projected back and **added** to the residual stream —
attention *writes* its findings rather than replacing anything.<Cite id="elhage2021" />

Switch heads in the selector: identical mechanism, different learned
projections — the subject of the next stage.
